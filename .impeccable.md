## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are CS2 fans, esports bettors, and data-driven analysts checking matches quickly before placing bets or tracking edges. They need high-signal information fast, but they also want the product to feel trustworthy, premium, and current.

### Brand Personality
Confident, sharp, premium.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first esports intelligence UI with cinematic depth, disciplined spacing, and premium editorial framing. The interface should feel more like a high-end trading terminal crossed with a modern esports broadcast package than a generic SaaS dashboard. Avoid gimmicky neon overload and avoid safe gray-box admin UI.

### Design Principles
1. Put the betting edge first. Important predictions, confidence, map context, and player context should be obvious within seconds.
2. Use strong hierarchy and disciplined density. Dense data is acceptable, but clutter is not.
3. Make premium feel structural. Depth should come from composition, typography, lighting, and motion, not cheap glow effects.
4. Treat player-related predictions as hero moments. Player images, props, and stat context should feel intentionally showcased.
5. Keep responsiveness elegant. Mobile should preserve the product's authority rather than collapsing into a cramped dashboard.
